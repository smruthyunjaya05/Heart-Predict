What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect your heart, including blood vessel diseases, heart rhythm problems, and congenital heart defects. It is one of the leading causes of death globally.

Common Symptoms

The most common symptoms include chest pain or tightness, shortness of breath, and fatigue. Patients may experience pain or numbness in their extremities, along with occasional heart palpitations or fluttering sensations in the chest.

‚ö°

Common Causes

Heart disease often develops due to various factors including high blood pressure, elevated cholesterol levels, smoking, and diabetes. Lifestyle factors such as obesity, poor diet, and lack of physical activity can significantly increase the risk.
